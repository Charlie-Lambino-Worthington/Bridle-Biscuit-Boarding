Spacious Stable Blocks


Each stall is equipped with state-of-the-art comfort features, ensuring your equine friend feels right at home.

Comfort Features:
Ventilation and Climate Control: Optimal ventilation is essential for maintaining a comfortable temperature inside the stalls. Built using materials like precast concrete panels for thermal mass, providing a cool respite.
Natural Light: Incorporating windows or skylights to allow natural light into the stalls, enhancing visibility and reducing reliance on artificial lights.
Flooring: Padded flooring can significantly increase comfort by minimizing pressure points and injuries.

Safety Measures:
Secure Enclosures: Sturdy doors and latches to prevent escapes and injuries as well as rounded corners and smooth surfaces to injury minimize risks.
Non-Toxic Materials: Built with select materials that are safe for horses, avoiding those that could cause harm if ingested or chewed.

Additional Amenities:
Feed and Water Systems: Automatic waterers and feeders simplify care routines and ensure horses have constant access to hydration and nutrition.
Ergonomic Design Elements: Including features that reduce strain on both horses and humans, such as adjustable stall sizes and ergonomic design elements.
Sustainable Materials: Built using recycled materials sourced sustainably to reduce the environmental impact of the stable blocks.

Extensive Grazing Land


We offer over 45 acres of rolling hills, shaded by mature trees, and equipped with auto-refilling water troughs and shelters, providing ample space for exploration and relaxation. Water Systems and Accessibility:

Auto-Refilling Water Troughs: We implement solar-powered auto-refilling water troughs to ensure livestock have constant access to fresh water. These systems are particularly beneficial in frigid weather, ensuring the water remains frost-free and accessible. Water troughs spread out to encourage even grazing distribution. This distance ensures the horses and donkeys won't have to walk too far to reach water, promoting healthier grazing habits and reducing the risk of overgrazing near water sources.

Shelter and Shading:
Shelters: Provide shaded areas to protect our equine friends from harsh sunlight and inclement weather. Shelters can also serve as resting spots, offering relief from the elements and encouraging them to explore different parts of the grazing land.

Environmental Considerations: we also utilize existing mature trees for natural shading and protection against wind, and are in the process of planting more trees strategically to enhance shade coverage and biodiversity within the grazing area.And using flexible grazing periods throughout multiple fields, setting recovery times based on seasonal changes and forage availability. This adaptive approach ensures optimal pasture health and livestock well-being.

Farriers specialize in the art and science of trimming and shoeing horses' hooves, a task that is crucial for maintaining proper foot alignment, supporting structures, and preventing lameness. Their expertise extends beyond just hoof care; they understand the interplay between hoof health and the horse's overall well-being, contributing to a holistic approach to animal care.

Having both professionals on-site full-time means that preventive care, routine maintenance, and emergency services are readily available. This continuity of care ensures that animals receive consistent, high-quality treatment throughout their stay. It fosters a sense of trust and security among owners, knowing that their animals are under the watchful eye of experts who are dedicated to their health and happiness. Moreover, it reflects a commitment to excellence in animal husbandry, emphasizing the importance of professional, experienced care in achieving optimal outcomes for both the animals and their human caregivers.

Observational benefits further highlight the walker's utility. This varied movement encourages coordination and reduces stiffness, leading to improved suppleness. Enhanced reflexes also reduce the horse's proneness to injury, making the Monarch’s Horse Walker a valuable tool for preventive care.

In conclusion, the inclusion of a Monarch’s Horse Walker in our stable is driven by its proven track record of enhancing horse health and performance. Its world-renowned status for quality, durability, and reliability positions it as an indispensable asset in our commitment to providing the highest standard of care for our horses.

Olympic size Sandschool


An Olympic-sized sand school offers a unique and beneficial environment for training and light exercises, catering to both horses and donkeys. This specialized facility is designed to provide a setting ideal for enhancing the physical fitness and mental agility of your equines. The sand surface, especially when deep, offers a fantastic workout for the horses, requiring them to engage more muscle groups to move effectively. This type of exercise is particularly beneficial for horses needing to build strength and endurance, as riding in sand is a great cardiovascular workout that strengthens various muscles that are less engaged on harder surfaces.

Moreover, sand is gentle on hard tissues such as bones and hooves, as well as on rider bodies. Horses with hard tissue lesions often thrive in soft sand environments, and unconditioned horses are less likely to suffer hard tissue injuries. This makes sand schools an ideal place for horses recovering from injuries or those prone to such issues, as the softer impact of sand can aid in rehabilitation.
